Career & Future Scope in Information Technology & Computer Engineering

    1: Network Administrator

    Network administrators are hired by businesses and organisations to oversee the smooth operation of their computer systems and networks. These professionals deal with network hardware, software and communication systems, and are responsible for their development, installation, maintenance, and providing support when needed. Network administrators are responsible for maintaining all network computer systems, regularly checking for updates, and monitoring the systems for any damage.

2: Machine Learning Engineer

Machine learning engineers are professionals who design, test and develop intelligent systems that can perform repetitive human tasks without any external command. They can train computers to perform tasks from prior programming without needing specific programming for each and every task. Machine learning engineers are in high demand by leading information technology companies around the globe today. Some of the best computer science colleges in Maharashtra offer cutting-edge programs in ML engineering.

3: Computer Network Architect

Computer network architects develop communication systems based on an organisation’s business needs. They also maintain networks, extranets and intranets based on business plans. One of their key responsibilities is to understand the organisation’s business plan well and implement strategic computer networks accordingly. Besides this they also have to update software regularly and integrate IT security patches at necessary places within the network.

Information security analysts are hired to protect a company’s sensitive and private information. The information could be pertaining to mission-critical plans or even personal details of employees; it is the job of information security analysts to safeguard this data using their expertise. A Diploma in Computer Science and Engineering after 12th can help you gain the right knowledge and skills to make it in the industry as an information security analyst.

A database administrator oversees the daily requirements of an organisation’s database. They have to keep the database software up-to-date, make necessary purchases to upgrade the same on a regular basis, employ relevant security measures, and suggest required modifications to current software used by the business. These professionals usually work with a team of IT professionals who keep an eye on the technological advancements to ensure the company’s database is up-to-date.
